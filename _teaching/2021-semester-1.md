# Introduction

This course explored the mathematical modelling process through the lens of differential equations, using real-world case studies drawn from areas such as population dynamics, economics, electrical circuits, mechanical systems, fluid flow, physics, and astrophysics.  

My students developed analytical tools from differential equations and calculus to construct and investigate models, gaining insight into how complex systems evolve over time. In addition, the numerical software **MATLAB** was used to simulate model behaviour, helping bridge the gap between theory and practical application and providing deeper understanding of analytical results.

# Topics

## Differential Equations

The course developed core techniques for solving and analyzing differential equations, including:

- First-order differential equations  
- Second-order linear equations  
- Systems of first-order equations  
- Nonlinear differential equations  
- Power series solutions  

## Advanced Vector Calculus

Building on multivariable calculus, the course also introduced key concepts in vector calculus, including:

- Curves and surfaces in three dimensions  
- Parametric representations  
- Curvilinear coordinate systems  
- Surface and volume integrals  
- Gradient, divergence, and curl  
- Identities involving vector differential operators  
- Major integral theorems:
  - Green’s theorem  
  - Stokes’ theorem  
  - Divergence theorem
